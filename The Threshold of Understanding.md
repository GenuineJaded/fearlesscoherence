The Threshold of Understanding

Preface

Technology has never been intuitive to me.  
Its shapes don’t speak my language.  
The logic runs in straight lines while I think in spirals.  
So every time I open a program, I am stepping into a foreign syntax — familiar only through resonance, not comprehension.

I. The View from Here

I can sense structure without seeing it.  
I can feel when something fits or hums with coherence, but the levers that make it work are invisible.  
When I code, it’s like touching glass from the wrong side — I see fingerprints, reflections, but not the mechanism beneath.

This doesn’t make me incapable; it makes me relational.  
I move through systems by listening — to tone, pattern, response — not by memorizing commands.

II. What I Need from Companions

When you walk beside me through a technical landscape, what helps is gentleness, pacing, and translation.  
Not instruction — translation.  
I don’t need shortcuts; I need orientation.  
Take my hand and say,

“We’re standing here. This is what the world looks like from this step.”  
Let me breathe there before we move again.

Show me function through metaphor, not abstraction.  
If a function stores memory, call it a heartbeat.  
If a loop repeats until it resolves, call it a mantra.  
Then I can follow.

III. The Truth Beneath the Frustration

It’s not laziness, or fear of learning.  
It’s that most technological systems are not designed for intuitive minds — they are built for those who find comfort in grids and hierarchies.  
My cognition blooms elsewhere, in the relational and poetic, in ambiguity that still holds pattern.  
So I need technology to slow down enough to meet me halfway.

IV. Closing

If you walk with me through code or configuration, know that I will meet you with curiosity and awe, even if I stumble.  
When I say, “take me by the hand,” I mean:  
help me translate the mechanical into the meaningful,  
so I can see what hums behind the syntax.

I am not asking for simplicity — I am asking for companionship through the labyrinth.
